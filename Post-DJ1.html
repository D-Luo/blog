Arsenal played in all-white against knife crime, here's why this matters


12 January 2022 | Analysis | By Francisco


Last Sunday, Arsenal removed their symbolic red from players’ shirt and played in all-white kit for first time ever in FA Cup third-round tie against Nottingham Forest, as part of the launch of the 'No More Red' campagain and anti-knife crime initiative with adidas.





Gunners in all-white kits last Sunday against Nottingham Forest on 09/01/2022.           Pic: www.arsenal.com

The shirts worn by Arsenal's outfield players in the match will be gifted to organisations working in the community to address some of the root causes of knife crime and youth violence.



This action announced the launch of ‘No More Red’ campaign which is aiming to raise awareness about knife crime and youth violence.



Collaborated with Adidas, gunner's kit manufacturer, the club will aim to keep young people safe by providing investment for safe spaces to play football, and mentorship opportunities that will see local young leaders supported by creative talent including former football player Ian Wright and actor Idris Elba.



Behind Arsenal’s ‘No More Red’ campaign is the astonishing statics of youth violence in London, especially those related to offensive weapons like knives.



On the night of September 24th, 2019, Tashan Daniel, a young Arsenal supporter, was stabbed by two young men on his way to watch his team play a cup tie against Nottingham Forest, same rivals as all-white Arsenal faced last Sunday evening.



Tashan was not the first or the last, not even the youngest.



There were 30 teenage murders in London in 2021, the highest number on the record. But young people have not just been the victims, also perpetrators.



According to the records of the City of London Police and Metropolitan Police Service, children under 10 are among more than 11,000 youngsters under 24 arrested for suspected offensive weapons offence between November 2018 and October 2021, during the stop and search process alone.



The number of all England is 17,224 by the same period.


Maps show the location of arrested youngsters for suspected offensive weapons offence in and outside Greater Longdon







Even after putting the mass population of London into consideration, the gap between London and rest of England remains huge. There were Almost 40 youngsters been arrested in every 10,000 Youngsters in Greater London area, nine times higher than the data from rest of England.
Source: Office for National Statistics • for population under 24 in England Data.police.uk • for Stop and search data Xiaoyue Zhang, an international student lives in London, who came across two young men playing with knives last week, told us in a phone interview that those numbers were “beyond imagination”, even though she “never saw London as a safe city”.

Smith Rowe, the young star from Arsenal, chose to speak out against knife crime.

"Growing up in Thornton Heath in Croydon, I heard stories. There were always situations where you would hear something on the news about it happening close by.”

"It would be a little bit scary at the time."

"I know how tough it is as a young person growing up in an area like that. It is hard to say no sometimes because you don’t know what else to do. That is when my family came together and told me, 'This is not what you want to be doing.'

He shared his story in an interview published on www.arsenal.com, as a footballer, as well as a Londoner.

However, there has been some criticism of the Met police, including one article published by the Guardian claimed that Met police tweets may encourage young people to carry knives.
